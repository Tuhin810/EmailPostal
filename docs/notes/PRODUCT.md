# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Email operators, marketing professionals, and system administrators who manage outbound email campaigns at scale. They are technically literate, need real-time visibility into delivery status, and expect precise control over sending behavior (domain rotation, throttling, per-domain daily limits). Their job is to deliver messages reliably, not to fight with the tool.

## Product Purpose

Mailer is a self-hosted, privacy-first bulk email platform. It lets teams configure multiple sending domains, compose campaigns, rotate senders intelligently, throttle delivery to protect reputation, and monitor every recipient's status. Success means emails arrive in the inbox, not the spam folder, at predictable rates without shared infrastructure risk.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Trusted. Efficient. This is a tool for professionals who value reliability over flash. The interface earns confidence through clarity and precision, not through decorative excess. Every element has a job; nothing is ornamental.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS cream: no boring white-plus-blue dashboard cliches. No hero-metric templates with gradient accents. No identical card grids with icon-heading-text repetition.
- Overly playful or colorful UIs. Mailer is a serious operations tool, not a consumer app.
- Dark theme as default. Not an avoidance of dark mode, but dark is not the identity. The scene drives the choice.

## Design Principles

1. **Earn trust through precision.** Every pixel, every label, every alignment communicates reliability. Ambiguity erodes confidence.
2. **Information density with clarity.** Operators need to see status, metrics, and actions at a glance. Hierarchy, not cramming.
3. **Actions are obvious.** The primary path on every screen is unmistakable. Secondary actions never compete.
4. **Workflow before chrome.** When a layout choice serves the task, it wins. When it just decorates, it's removed.
5. **Consistency is safety.** The same pattern means the same thing everywhere. Surprise belongs nowhere near email delivery.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. Sufficient color contrast on all text and interactive elements. Focus indicators visible on all controls. Reduced motion respected.
